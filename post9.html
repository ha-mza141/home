Efficient Client Onboarding

Published: June 12, 2025 6 min read

Client onboarding is your first impression—and it can make or break the relationship. A seamless, professional process builds trust and sets the stage for long-term success. Here’s how to set up a smooth onboarding experience using QuickBooks and simple tools.

1. Use a Client Intake Form

Create a secure Google Form or use onboarding templates to collect necessary client details like business info, EIN, services needed, and access credentials.

2. Share a Welcome Packet

Include your services overview, communication policies, software you’ll use (like QuickBooks), and what documents clients need to provide upfront.

3. Schedule a Kickoff Call

A brief Zoom call helps clarify scope, timelines, and expectations. Use this opportunity to answer questions and explain how reporting and billing will work.

4. Set Up in QuickBooks

Add your client as a customer or set up their QuickBooks account if you’ll manage their books. Configure chart of accounts, tax settings, and invoicing details.

5. Automate Tasks

Use recurring invoices, payment reminders, and task checklists to streamline your workflow from day one.

Pro Tip: Keep all onboarding steps in a shared Google Sheet to track progress and never miss a step.
