Inventory Management in QuickBooks

Published: June 12, 2025 7 min read

QuickBooks offers robust inventory management features that help you track stock levels, cost of goods sold (COGS), and inventory valuation. This is ideal for small businesses managing physical products.

Enable Inventory Tracking

Go to Account Settings → Sales → Products and Services, and turn on "Track Quantity on Hand". This activates inventory features within your QuickBooks account.

Add Inventory Items

When adding a new item, include the SKU, cost, initial quantity, and reorder point. QuickBooks will automatically adjust inventory levels when products are sold or purchased.

Monitor Reorder Points

Set reorder thresholds to avoid stockouts. QuickBooks will alert you when inventory dips below a specified level.

Run Inventory Reports

Use reports like Inventory Valuation Summary and Stock Status by Item to stay on top of your product performance and turnover rates.

Integration with Sales

QuickBooks links inventory with invoices and sales receipts, making real-time tracking easy. You can also integrate with e-commerce platforms for seamless data syncing.
