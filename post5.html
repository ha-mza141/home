QuickBooks Payroll Setup Guide

Published: June 12, 2025 8 min read

Setting up payroll in QuickBooks Online is a crucial step for any small business that employs staff. With the right setup, you can automate salary calculations, tax filings, and direct deposits—saving time and reducing errors.

Step 1: Choose the Right Payroll Plan

QuickBooks offers multiple payroll plans: Core, Premium, and Elite. Assess your business needs and choose the plan that supports your desired level of HR and tax automation.

Step 2: Enter Company Payroll Details

Set up your company’s EIN (Employer Identification Number), business address, and banking details for direct deposits. You’ll also enter state and federal tax information here.

Step 3: Add Employees

Enter each employee’s personal details including address, Social Security number, W-4 information, pay schedule, and payment method. You can also track paid time off, benefits, and deductions.

Step 4: Set Up Payroll Schedules

Create custom pay schedules (weekly, bi-weekly, monthly) based on how often you pay your employees. QuickBooks will remind you before every due date.

Step 5: Connect Your Bank

Connect your business checking account to enable direct deposit. Make sure you verify your account through the QuickBooks verification process (usually two small test deposits).

Step 6: Run Your First Payroll

Once your employees and schedules are set, you can run your first payroll. QuickBooks calculates federal and state taxes, deducts benefits, and sends payments to employees.

Step 7: File Taxes Automatically

Depending on your plan, QuickBooks can handle tax filings, generate W-2s and 1099s, and even pay payroll taxes on your behalf. Make sure you enable auto-tax features.

Tips for Smooth Payroll Management

  • Double-check employee info for accuracy.
  • Set up reminders for form submissions and filings.
  • Review payroll reports regularly to catch errors early.
  • Use the QuickBooks mobile app to run payroll on the go.
