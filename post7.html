Year-End Accounting Checklist

Published: June 12, 2025 6 min read

Before closing the books for the year, it’s essential to follow a proper accounting checklist. This ensures you stay compliant, avoid surprises during tax season, and start the new year with accurate financials.

1. Reconcile All Bank Accounts

Match every transaction in QuickBooks with your bank and credit card statements. Any discrepancies should be investigated and resolved.

2. Review Profit and Loss Statement

Analyze income and expenses to ensure everything is categorized properly. This will help maximize deductions and avoid misclassification.

3. Double-Check Balance Sheet

Ensure assets, liabilities, and equity are up to date. Confirm loan balances, depreciation entries, and undeposited funds are accurate.

4. Verify Payroll and Contractor Payments

Ensure W-2s and 1099s are complete and correct. If you used QuickBooks Payroll, most forms are auto-generated and ready for download.

5. Capture Last-Minute Expenses

Enter any outstanding receipts, bills, or reimbursable expenses before closing the books. This ensures you claim everything you’re entitled to.

6. Back Up Your Financial Data

Download reports and save them securely. Year-end reports are critical for compliance and loan applications.

7. Close the Books

Once everything is reviewed, lock your books to prevent changes. In QuickBooks, go to Settings → Accounts and Settings → Advanced → Close the books.
